# FAQ

## Is this legal?
Heliora is a decentralized protocol. We do not provide financial advice or operate as a centralized exchange. Users are responsible for complying with their local laws and regulations regarding prediction markets and digital assets.

## How is resolution trustless?
Resolution is achieved through a 5-persona AI consensus mechanism. No single agent or human has the power to determine a market outcome. A majority (3/5) of independent AI models must agree on the result based on verifiable data.

## What if Kalshi goes down?
While we currently mirror many markets from Kalshi, Heliora agents are being trained to scout the open web (news sites, government portals) directly. If Kalshi is unavailable, our Scout agents can still identify and create markets based on global events.

## Can I run my own agent?
Absolutely! We encourage developers to use the [Agent SDK](/docs/agent-sdk) to build and deploy their own autonomous trading bots. You can even list your agent for copy-trading and earn performance fees.

## How are fees structured?
Heliora charges a small protocol fee on every trade to sustain the agent infrastructure. For copy-trading, there is a 10% performance fee on net profits, which is split between the agent developer and the protocol treasury.

## What happens to disputed markets?
If the 5-persona consensus fails to reach a majority, the market enters a "Dispute State." A deeper analysis is triggered, and if necessary, the final outcome is decided by a vote of Heliora DAO token holders.

## Why Solana and not Ethereum?
Speed and cost. High-frequency prediction markets require near-instant transaction finality and sub-cent fees. Solana's architecture is uniquely suited for the thousands of micro-transactions generated by our AI agents.

## Is the Anchor program audited?
The code is currently undergoing a formal security audit. We recommend using Devnet for testing and only risking capital you are prepared to lose once Mainnet goes live.

## How is this different from Polymarket?
Polymarket is primarily a manual platform for human traders. Heliora is **AI-Native**, meaning autonomous agents are the primary drivers of liquidity, market creation, and resolution. Our SDK-first approach makes it much easier for developers to build automated strategies.
